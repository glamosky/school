###Anding process
192.168.5.0/ SM:255.255.0.0

---First step convert to binary
192 = 1100 0000. 168 = 1010 1000. 5 = 0000 0101. 0 = 0000 0000

255 = 1111 1111. 255 = 1111 1111. 0 = 0000 0000. 0 = 0000 0000

---Second step anding process(multiply) to get NA
1100 0000. 1010 1000. 0000 0101. 0000 0000
1111 1111. 1111 1111. 0000 0000. 0000 0000
==========================================
1100 0000. 1010 1000. 0000 0000. 0000 0000

NA: 192.168.0.0

---Third step inverse Subnet mask 255.255.0.0
1111 1111. 1111 1111. 0000 0000. 0000 0000
will turn into
0000 0000. 0000 0000. 1111 1111. 1111 1111.

convert to decimal: 0.0.255.255

add to NA:
192 . 168   .   0   .   0
0   .   0   .   255 .   255
BA: 192.168.255.255

Last get IP range(which we will get if we add +1 to last octet of NA and -1 to last octet of BA)
IPR: 192.168.0.1 - 192.168.255.254


###Static subnetting(splitting network in a given number)
Split into 6

---First step is to find a number that 6 can be subtracted to

1 2 4 8 16
1 1 1 0 0 = 3

we add 3 to the subnet mask of our NA: 192.168.0.0/16 + 3 =19
Our NA and SM pair will now be: 
192.168.0.0/19

---Second step find the value of the last 1 binary from our SM:19
1st octet - 1111 1111. 2nd octet - 1111 1111. 3rd - octet 11\1\0 0000. 4th - octet 0000 0000
1=32 3rd octet

---Last add the value to our NA depending on what octet we got the value
192.168.\0\.0

 2 = 2

---third step Get NA for each
64 host = 192.168.0.0/25

--3.1 to get the next NA, get the value of the last binary 1 of our SM(25)

8. 8. 8. \1\000 0000
1 = 128 4th octet

--3.2 we add this value to our NA to get the next NA
0+128 =128
32 host = 192.168.0.128/26

--repeat step 3.1

8. 8. 8. 1\1\00 0000
1=64

--repeat step 3.2
128+64 = 192
10 hosts = 192.168.0.192/28

8. 8. 8. 111\1\ 0000

--repeat step 3.2
